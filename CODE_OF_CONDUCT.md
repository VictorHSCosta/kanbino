# Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

In the interest of fostering an open and welcoming environment, we as contributors and maintainers pledge to making participation in our project and our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to creating a positive environment include:

* Using welcoming and inclusive language
* Being respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences
* Gracefully accepting constructive criticism
* Focusing on what is best for the community
* Showing empathy towards other community members

Examples of unacceptable behavior by participants include:

* The use of sexualized language or imagery and unwelcome sexual attention or advances
* Trolling, insulting/derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
* Public or private harassment
* Publishing others' private information, such as a physical or electronic address, without explicit permission
* Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

## Our Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying the standards of acceptable behavior and are expected to take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any instances of unacceptable behavior.

Project maintainers have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned to this Code of Conduct, or to ban temporarily or permanently any contributor for other behaviors that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies both within project spaces and in public spaces when an individual is representing the project or its community. Examples of representing a project or community include using an official project e-mail address, posting via an official social media account, or acting as an appointed representative at an online or offline event. Representation of a project may be further defined and clarified by project maintainers.

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported by contacting the project team at victorhenriquecosta23@gmail.com. All complaints will be reviewed and investigated and will result in a response that is deemed necessary and appropriate to the circumstances. The project team is obligated to maintain confidentiality with regard to the reporter of an incident. Further details of specific enforcement policies may be posted separately.

Project maintainers who do not follow or enforce the Code of Conduct in good faith may face temporary or permanent repercussions as determined by other members of the project's leadership.

## Additional Guidelines for Gemkanbino

### Communication Standards

#### GitHub Issues and Pull Requests
- **Be Specific**: When reporting issues, provide clear steps to reproduce the problem, include system information (OS, Ruby version, gem version), and error messages.
- **Use Descriptive Titles**: Make it easy for others to understand the issue or PR at a glance.
- **Be Patient**: Remember that maintainers are volunteers and may need time to respond.
- **Stay Focused**: Keep discussions on-topic and focused on the technical aspects of the project.

#### Code Contributions
- **Follow Style Guidelines**: Adhere to the existing code style and Ruby conventions.
- **Write Tests**: Include tests for new features and ensure existing tests pass.
- **Document Changes**: Update documentation for any code changes that affect user-facing behavior.
- **Small, Focused Changes**: Keep pull requests focused on a single feature or fix.

#### Discussions and Reviews
- **Assume Good Intent**: Approach discussions with the assumption that contributors have good intentions.
- **Be Constructive**: Provide feedback that helps improve the code or documentation.
- **Acknowledge Contributions**: Recognize and appreciate the time and effort contributors invest.
- **Learn and Teach**: Be open to learning from others and sharing knowledge.

### Specific Behaviors

#### Encouraged Behaviors
- **Helpful Responses**: When answering questions, provide clear, actionable answers.
- **Welcoming Newcomers**: Make extra effort to help new contributors get started.
- **Documentation Improvements**: Contribute to improving documentation for better clarity.
- **Code Reviews**: Participate constructively in code reviews.
- **Issue Triage**: Help categorize and prioritize issues.

#### Discouraged Behaviors
- **Gatekeeping**: Making others feel unwelcome or questioning their right to contribute.
- **Dismissive Responses**: Ignoring or belittling valid questions or concerns.
- **Unrealistic Expectations**: Demanding immediate responses or feature implementations.
- **Self-Promotion**: Excessive promotion of personal projects or services.
- **Off-Topic Discussions**: Steering conversations away from project-related topics.

### Conflict Resolution

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior:

1. **Direct Communication**: When appropriate and safe, consider addressing the issue directly with the person involved.
2. **Report to Maintainers**: Contact the project maintainers at victorhenriquecosta23@gmail.com with details of the incident.
3. **Provide Context**: Include relevant information such as screenshots, links, and descriptions of the behavior.
4. **Maintain Privacy**: Reports will be kept confidential to protect all parties involved.

### Enforcement Process

1. **Initial Review**: Project maintainers will review the report within 7 days.
2. **Investigation**: A fair investigation will be conducted, considering all perspectives.
3. **Action**: Appropriate action will be taken, which may include:
   - **Warning**: A private warning to clarify expectations
   - **Temporary Suspension**: Temporary removal from project spaces
   - **Permanent Ban**: Permanent removal from project spaces for serious violations
4. **Follow-up**: The situation will be monitored to ensure the behavior doesn't continue.

### Appeals

If you believe enforcement action has been taken unfairly:
1. Contact the project maintainers within 14 days of the enforcement action.
2. Provide a clear explanation of why you believe the action was unfair.
3. Include any relevant context or additional information.
4. The maintainers will review the appeal and respond within 14 days.

### Positive Recognition

We want to recognize and celebrate positive contributions to our community:
- **Helpful Contributors**: Members who consistently provide valuable assistance
- **Welcoming Behavior**: Individuals who make newcomers feel included
- **Constructive Feedback**: Contributors who provide thoughtful, helpful reviews
- **Documentation Heroes**: Those who significantly improve project documentation

### Language and Inclusivity

#### English Language
While our testing documentation is in Portuguese, our primary language for code comments, issues, and discussions is English. This ensures the widest possible participation.

#### Translation Support
We welcome contributions that help make the project more accessible:
- Translating documentation to other languages
- Providing localized error messages
- Creating region-specific examples and tutorials

#### Accessibility
We strive to make our project accessible to people with diverse abilities:
- Use descriptive alt text for images in documentation
- Ensure sufficient color contrast in UI elements
- Provide keyboard navigation where applicable
- Use clear, simple language in documentation

### Professional Development

This code of conduct is designed to help everyone grow professionally:

#### Learning Opportunities
- **Mentorship**: Experienced members are encouraged to mentor newcomers
- **Code Reviews**: Treat reviews as learning opportunities for all parties
- **Knowledge Sharing**: Share insights and discoveries with the community

#### Growth Mindset
- **Open to Feedback**: Be receptive to constructive criticism
- **Continuous Improvement**: Always look for ways to improve code, documentation, and processes
- **Celebrate Learning**: Recognize that mistakes are opportunities for growth
